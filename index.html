Jobin Jose graduated from one of the prestigious engineering colleges in Kerala, the Government College of Engineering at Kannur. During his engineering days, he used to enjoy having academic discussions with his juniors, which deepened his passion for teaching. After completion of his BTech degree, he started his career as a lecturer at the College of Engineering, Thalassery where he taught undergraduate engineering students. Later he shifted to AWH Engineering College, Calicut, where he continued to teach. College of Engineering, Kannur. Along with the regular academic duties, he happily took on various roles as an assistant resident tutor and assistant placement coordinator. He also was instrumental in forming various clubs in the college, namely coding club and radio club. He also engaged in mentoring various other hobby clubs related to movie club, robotic club (ROBOCEK), and innovative electronics club. He was involved in coordinating GATE coaching sessions, organizing National Service Scheme camps, and facilitating various industry-institute interaction workshops for students. For a brief period of time in his career, he was part of a start-up that focused on manufacturing automatic kitchen utilities. He returned to Govt. College of Engineering Kannur in 2018 August and resumed teaching. He was then involved in facilitating various national technical exhibitions and a TEDx event in college.

From 2020 onwards he is acting as a mentor and board advisor in Deepflow Technologies Pvt Ltd, an agri-tech start-up company working for farmers who are cultivating spices and cash crops in India, aiming at improving their quality of production and financial gains. Since April 2021 he has worked with the Distance Education Unit of CMC Vellore as an external service provider, where he has been involved in administrative and technical tasks related to content creation, teaching sessions, student support, and management of the Post Graduate Diploma in Rehabilitation Medicine (PGDRM) and Post Graduate Diploma in Mental Health (PGDMH) courses run by the Distance Education Unit, CMC Vellore. He was also involved in academic tasks related to content creation and contextualization of materials for the nonmedical learners in the PGDRM course.

His areas of interest include embedded system design, control system engineering, and the use of educational technology for the simplification and contextualization of teaching methodologies. He strives to be part of an environment which provides ample scope for career advancement and mutual growth.
